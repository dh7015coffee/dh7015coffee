Influencing Factors
  • Climatic conditions: The growth of coffee depends on specific climatic conditions, such as suitable temperature and precipitation. Extreme weather events such as frosts, droughts and floods can affect coffee production, thus affecting supply and prices.
  • Pests and diseases: Coffee pests and diseases, such as coffee leaf rust and coffee peach bug, can significantly reduce coffee production and affect supply.
  • Consumer preferences: Consumer preferences for coffee influence demand. With the rise of the third wave of coffee, consumer demand for high-quality and uniquely flavored coffee has increased, driving the development of the specialty coffee market.
  • Economic conditions: Economic conditions directly affect the export capacity of coffee-producing countries and the demand of foreign markets. In times of economic prosperity, consumer purchasing power increases and demand for high-quality coffee usually increases. Conversely, in a recession, consumers may cut back on discretionary purchases. For example, during the 2008 global financial crisis, many countries experienced a severe economic downturn and global demand for coffee fell.
  • Exchange rate: Exchange rate fluctuations can significantly affect the profitability of coffee exports. When a producing country's currency depreciates, its coffee becomes cheaper for foreign buyers, potentially increasing exports. A stronger currency, however, would make coffee more expensive for foreign buyers, potentially reducing exports.
  • International trade policies: International trade policies, including tariffs, quotas and trade agreements, also play an important role in coffee exports. Open trade policies and low tariffs help boost coffee exports, while protectionist policies and high tariffs may limit exports.
